IA

Previously, the website relied on a long, continuous scroll. While this pattern can work well for mobile, it is less effective for a tax and accounting website, where users typically want to locate specific information quickly rather than scroll through an entire page. Additionally, the navigation bar did not reflect all of the site’s content sections. This created a disconnect between what was available on the page and what was represented in the navigation. If certain sections are not important enough to be surfaced in the menu, their necessity within the page itself becomes questionable.

I introduced a straightforward sitemap in which the navigation bar provides direct access to all primary content. This ensured that users could quickly locate key information without relying on continuous scrolling. To reduce redundancy and clarify hierarchy, I consolidated overlapping sections—for example, merging Our Services and Additional Services—and removed low-value or repetitive content, such as duplicated Yelp review sections. These changes resulted in a cleaner structure, clearer priorities, and a more efficient navigation experience.

CTA

Clear calls to action are critical for conversion but the original website suffered from overexposure. A modal prompt appeared twice—once immediately after the landing section, which already included a primary CTA, and again at the very end of the page, where a contact form was also present. In addition, a phone number was displayed in the navigation bar alongside a dedicated Contact Us menu item. This redundancy created visual clutter, disrupted user flow, and diluted the importance of overlathe primary action by presenting too many competing options at once.
